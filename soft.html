Soft Skills: The Human Element in Data Science

Exploring the Interpersonal Skills That Complement Technical Expertise

Communication: The Art of Clarity and Collaboration

When a new executive order came into effect, I identified potential reporting gaps in our compliance system. I proactively communicated these concerns to relevant stakeholders, including compliance officers and senior management. By facilitating a workshop to explain the new regulation and its implications, I ensured that all stakeholders were on the same page. My efforts led to a seamless update, maintaining our adherence to the new executive order.

Problem-Solving: Bridging Perspectives and Finding Solutions

In a cross-departmental meeting, there were strong disagreements between the operations team and the control assurance team regarding the interpretation of an executive order. I actively listened to both sides and then restated the key points of contention in a neutral and objective manner. By facilitating a discussion and asking clarifying questions, I helped reach a consensus that streamlined our operational processes. This ability to mediate and problem-solve has been invaluable in maintaining team cohesion.

Collaboration: Uniting Diverse Talents Towards a Common Goal

I was tasked with leading a project to implement a new AML process. The project team included individuals from IT, control assurance, and operations. I ensured open communication and regular check-ins with each team member, leveraging their specific expertise to achieve project goals. By fostering collaboration and leveraging diverse perspectives, we successfully implemented the KYC platform on schedule, enhancing our ability to identify and mitigate customer onboarding risks.

Leadership: Empowering Others and Driving Change

As a senior member of the financial crime control team, I mentored junior colleagues, providing guidance and support in their AML investigations. I also volunteered to lead training sessions on new regulations and best practices for fraud detection. By sharing my knowledge and experience, I contributed to the development of my colleagues' skills and expertise, enhancing our team's overall effectiveness in combating financial crime.

Adaptability: Embracing Change and Continuous Learning

With the introduction of new artificial intelligence (AI) tools in transaction monitoring, I recognized the need to adapt my skill set. I enrolled in online courses focused on AI applications in financial crime control and sought opportunities to learn from colleagues who were already using AI tools. This proactive approach to learning has allowed me to remain competitive in the field and contribute more effectively to financial crime prevention efforts.
